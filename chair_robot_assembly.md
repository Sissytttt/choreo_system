Assembly instructions
<details>
  <summary>Chair hardware(Click to expand/collapse)</summary>


1. Seperate two halves of hoverboard
    (this is only necessary to do if the width of the chair legs does not permit the hoverboard to stay intact)
   
   <img src="https://github.com/IRL-CT/mobilehri2023/blob/dancerobot/images/IMG_9062.jpg"  width="300"/>
   
   Remove this clip on each side, pulling on the tip using pliers.

2. Insert aluminum tubing across center of the hoverboard, to help keep the two halves of the hoverboard aligned. (This is not affixed, just pressed in.)
   
      <img src="https://github.com/IRL-CT/mobilehri2023/blob/dancerobot/images/IMG_1386.jpg"  width="300"/>

3. Use 2 extruded aluminum t-slot rails across the flat side of the hoverboard chassis to create wider hoverboard chassis, using t-slot bolts and drop-in nuts.
   
   <img src="https://github.com/IRL-CT/mobilehri2023/blob/dancerobot/images/IMG_1387.jpg"  width="300"/>

4. Attach the back boots (white 3d printed parts) on the hoverboard loosely, also using the t-slot bolts and nuts.
5. Attach the chair legs into the boots, and adjust the hoverboard assembly laterally to accommodate the chair width. Tighten everything.

   <img src="https://github.com/IRL-CT/mobilehri2023/blob/dancerobot/images/IMG_1323.jpg" width="300"/>

7. Attach front boots to front legs of chair. There is a set screw in back that you can use to secure the boots.

8. Add the magnetic knife block under the seat of the chair. Its role is just to weight the front of the chair down.
   
 <img src="https://github.com/IRL-CT/mobilehri2023/blob/dancerobot/images/IMG_1321.jpg"  width="300"/>

 2. Use these parts to connect the control wires from the hoverboard motors to the o-drive: 

  <img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/81GpyaVT5VL._SS284_.jpg" />
  <img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/71G0-aKf0qL._SS284_.jpg" />

  3. Use these parts to connect the 36V battery to the o-drive:
    <img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/61pAg2exrIL._SS284_.jpg" />

4. Connect the o-drive board to the Raspberry Pi using a usb cable.

5. Use a usb cable to connect the Raspberry Pi to the Usb battery.

6. Use Dual lock to attach the parts to the hoverboard chassis.

7. Use zipties to manage the wires
